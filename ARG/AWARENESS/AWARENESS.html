What is Addiction?

Addiction is a complex brain disorder characterized by compulsive engagement in rewarding stimuli despite adverse consequences. It's not a moral failing or lack of willpowerâ€”it's a medical condition that affects brain chemistry and behavior.

The disease affects millions of people worldwide, altering brain circuits related to reward, stress, and self-control. Understanding addiction as a treatable medical condition is crucial for reducing stigma and encouraging those affected to seek help.

Recovery is possible with proper treatment, support, and understanding. Every person's journey is unique, and with the right resources and community support, individuals can reclaim their lives and build lasting wellness.

Common Myths vs Facts

Myth

Addiction is a choice and lack of willpower

Fact

Addiction is a chronic brain disease that affects decision-making and impulse control

Myth

Treatment doesn't work for most people

Fact

Treatment is effective and recovery rates are similar to other chronic diseases

Myth

You have to hit rock bottom before getting help

Fact

Early intervention leads to better outcomes and prevents further damage
